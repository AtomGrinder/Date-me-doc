Me

Why am I single?

  • My work life (sea below) has forced me to relocate often.
  • I used to be insecure about expressing romantic attraction, although this has been worked through.
  • My face is not very expressive, and I seem much colder than I actually am to a lot of people. I convey strong emotion much more with verbal explanation than with any visual cue.
  • The places I’ve lived are not bustling with like-minded people.
  • I am close friends with an ex-girlfriend, and have many female friends. Staying on each other’s sofas is normal for me, but this has caused some women a lot of jealousy/anxiety.

Work life

For five years after graduating, I served as a warfare officer in the Royal Navy. Much of this was spent on Microsoft PowerPoint and Outlook, with short bursts of periscope drills and high-pressure leadership duties. Key info to take from this:

  • My identity as a leader is important to me.
  • I’m much less conservative politically than the military stereotype. If you have a rule not to date military men, I’m fairly confident it’s for reasons that don’t apply here.
  • That said, my personal culture has been influenced significantly along non-political lines.
    • I keep my home fairly tidy.
    • People not doing what they said they would do angers me more than it does most people.
  • I greet everyone with “Ahoy”.

Since leaving, I’ve become the second-in-command of a research nonprofit working to improve the security of frontier AI deployments. Cybercriminals and authoritarian governments stealing transformatively powerful AI seems really bad, so we’re trying to make that prohibitively difficult ahead of time.

Character traits

  • Discipline, drive, and executive function
  • Very low in neuroticism and anxiety, with a stable mood (I’ve got graphs lol)

Often, questions just don’t appear in my head while chatting. If I’m choosing to spend time with you, assume you have a free pass to tell me anything you want about what’s going on in your life, your interests, anything.

  • I’m becoming more disagreeable and willing to challenge people as I age. I’d like to believe I keep this constructive and make sure not to leave others feeling attacked. If your tortilla policy is mental, or I think a key part of your philosophy is wrong, you’ll hear about it.
    • For big stuff, I make sure to check that the other person has the emotional energy for a challenging conversation. It’s important that you do the same with me.

Daily routines

  • I am more of a morning person; staying up late into the night without a good reason is not a thing for me
  • I reach full alertness and energy exactly 20 minutes after getting up. Unless I melatoned for my sins the previous night… the sleep inertia is bad.
  • I rarely suffer from low energy and nap only infrequently
  • My meals are very big, and I hardly snack
  • I abhor “killing time” at home and prefer to spend time intentionally. This most frequently comes up in relationships around partners wanting to spend lots of time watching TV/film. If you want me to do this, it’s best to sell me on why that show in particular is worth a deliberate decision to watch it. Just putting something on as a low-effort way to spend an evening will not fly.
  • Exercise intensely 3 days on, 1 day off
  • Each night, I record what I did that day in a “Memory Book” and have every single day since 2018-01-01. Be prepared to feature.

Romance & the Bedroom

  • Pretty happy with vanilla stuff, but I have discovered a dominant side and would like to explore this more
  • I’m not that kinky
  • I’m not sure I would have believed it before, but I’ve unlocked a part of my mind that experiences sex in ways similar to women: erogenous zones and mind-splitting orgasms that last several seconds and aren’t related to ejaculation
  • High libido and I actively want near-daily sex, but my desire to go for multiple rounds can be greatly hampered by not having eaten enough or physical tiredness. You’ll generally get a lot more out of me by starting early in the evening, or earlier in the day, with a coffee
  • … friends mocking me for going to the Roomba recharge station

I’ve had one relationship lasting a year, a handful of <4-month situationships, and a fair number of short-term flings or one-off encounters. I’m in touch and on good terms with many of these people, and don’t hold grudges against any. None of these were “bad” breakups, and I have never fought with a romantic partner. My longest relationship was polyamorous for the second half, which taught me a lot about how I would experience such dynamics in future. It also raised my bar for communication and working through jealousy a lot.

I like to take relationships quickly and am comfortable living together earlier than most, I think. During early dating, I want to get all our potential incompatibilities on the table ASAP and discuss what tests our connection needs to pass before we both feel safe calling it a relationship. I’m happy “going with the flow” for casual dating, but do not do this for serious dating.

As I grow close with people, I can become an absolute Snuggle Bear™, but this is dependent on chemistry.

You

Basic info

There are very few unequivocal green and red flags for me, and many different ways I can be attracted to a person. Most of my red flags are fairly common: jealous, controlling, unable to accept they are at fault, and boring. I list ones specific to me below.

Green flags

  • Clear and open communication
  • Confidence/assertiveness
  • Self-initiated passion projects, ideally nerdy ones
  • Morally motivated, seeking to make the world better
  • Has done really hard stuff
  • High IQ and at least middling EQ
  • Outdoorsy / not dependent on creature comforts
  • Craft hobbies
  • Aged 23–33
  • Muscular/athletic figure
  • Large and/or perky breasts

Red flags

  • Actively religious or superstitious
  • Not vegan — I’m open to dating non-vegans, but for it to go anywhere I expect a partner to engage with my philosophy on this with an open mind. I’d do the same for harm-reduction causes important to them.
  • Lack of interest in their health, fitness, and longevity

Work life

I want to date people who impress me, and a big part of that will usually come from their career goals. Ambition is helpful, but at a minimum, I want a partner to know who they are and what they are working towards. The minutiae of this are not so important to me, and there are very few professions that constitute red flags. Essentially, I want to be impressed by a woman’s grit. Tell me how hard you’ve worked and what you can overcome.

Finances

I have an egalitarian attitude to dating, including our money. If I earn more than a partner, I am happy to pay proportionately more for the things we do. However, it is a hard line in the sand that I will not be expected to pay for things because I am a man. With that said, I don’t have any expectations for a partner’s income or financial situation, provided that they can take care of themselves.

The Bedroom

I would like for a partner to be of a similarly high sex drive and committed to this remaining an exciting activity. Ideally, a warm, empty field in the summer would be a perfectly acceptable opportunity to get it on. Eagerness to understand my weird tantric capabilities and consensual power imbalance would be much appreciated. There’s not much more of a filter I want to apply here; the rest can be figured out with dating.
